Module 01

Prompting &
Chat Best Practices

The single most important skill in the AI era. Everything else builds on this.

Core Idea

Prompting Is Just Clear Communication

AI can't read your mind. The clearer you are about what you want, who you are, and what "good" looks like, the better the result.

❌ Vague

"Write an essay about AI."

✓ Clear

"Write a 500-word essay for a high school class on how AI is changing how small companies work. Use 2-3 real examples. Conversational tone, no jargon."

The Recipe

4 Ingredients of a Great Prompt

01

Role

Who should the AI be? "Act as a science teacher" / "Be a skeptical editor."

02

Task

What do you actually want? Be specific. One job per prompt is best.

03

Context

Background it needs. Who's the audience? What's the situation?

04

Format

What should the output look like? Bullet list? Essay? Table? Length?

Pro Moves

Things That Level You Up Fast

  • Iterate, don't restart.If the first answer's not great, say what's off. "Make it shorter." "Less formal." "Add an example."
  • Show, don't just tell.Paste an example of the style or format you want. AI matches patterns.
  • Ask AI to think out loud."Walk me through your reasoning step by step." Catches mistakes.
  • Ask for options, not just one answer."Give me 3 different angles I could take." Then pick.
  • Always fact-check important stuff.AI is confident even when it's wrong. Trust but verify.
Module 1 · Hands-On

Try It Yourself (30–45 min)

Open ChatGPT, Claude, or whatever tool Derick gives you access to. Try all five:

  1. Get AI to explain quantum computing like you're 10. Then like you're an expert.
  2. Give it a paragraph you wrote and ask for 3 different rewrites — formal, funny, super short.
  3. Ask it to help you plan a 3-day trip somewhere you've never been.
  4. Have it interview you about a topic until it can write a good essay about you.
  5. Find one thing it gets wrong. Push back. Watch it correct itself (or double down — both are useful!).
Module 02

Co-Work
with AI

Stop using AI like a search engine. Start using it like a teammate.

The Shift

From Question-and-Answer to Collaboration

Most people use AI for one-shot questions. The real power is in long conversations where AI becomes part of your workflow.

📝

Thought Partner

"Help me think through this." Brainstorm, pressure-test ideas, find blind spots.

🔍

Research Assistant

"What are the leading approaches to X? Pros and cons?" Summarize, compare, dig in.

✍️

Writing Partner

Outline → draft → edit → polish, all in one conversation. Better than blank page.

🎯

Coach

"Quiz me on this." "Review my work like a tough teacher." Levels you up.

The Secret

Give It Context. Always.

The difference between a generic answer and a brilliant one is usually how much context you fed in.

Generic prompt
"Help me write a college essay."
Context-rich prompt
"I'm a high school senior applying to engineering programs. I love robotics and built a working drone last summer. The prompt is: 'Describe a moment that shaped you.' Help me brainstorm 3 angles I could take — what's a story that shows curiosity and grit without being cheesy?"

The second prompt gets you a real answer. The first gets you Wikipedia.

Module 2 · Hands-On

Co-Work Through a Real Task (60 min)

Pick a real thing from your life — a paper that's due, a presentation, a decision you're trying to make. Don't ask AI to do it. Work through it together.

  1. Start by explaining the situation in detail — like briefing a coworker on day one.
  2. Ask it to ask you clarifying questions before giving any advice.
  3. Brainstorm options. Get it to argue both sides.
  4. Make your decision, then have it help you execute the first step.
  5. Save the chat. You'll be surprised how useful it is to look back at it.
Module 03

Vibe Coding
& Low-Code

Build software by describing what you want. This is one of the biggest shifts of the AI era.

The New Way

You Don't Need to Code to Build Software

"Vibe coding" — coined in 2026 — means describing what you want in plain English and letting AI write the actual code. Low-code/no-code tools take it even further: drag, drop, done.

The Old Way

Learn a language for 6 months → finally build a simple app → spend hours fixing bugs.

The Vibe Way

"Make me a webpage that tracks how much water I drink each day with a streak counter." → working app in 20 minutes.

This whole curriculum you're looking at? Vibe-coded. The Tech Vision deck Derick uses? Same.

The Toolkit

Tools You Should Try

💬

Claude / ChatGPT (Artifacts & Canvas)

Best starting point. Ask for an HTML page, get a working preview right in chat.

⚡

v0.dev

Describe a UI, get a polished React component. Vercel's tool. Free tier exists.

🛠️

Replit / Lovable / Bolt

Whole app-builders. Type a prompt, get a deployed website.

📊

Airtable / Glide / Softr

Low-code databases & apps. Spreadsheet → real app, no code.

Start with one. Get a small win. Then expand.

Module 3 · Project (2–3 hours)

🚀 Build & Ship Something Real

Pick one and actually ship it. Send Derick the link.

  1. Personal Dashboard — a webpage that shows your school schedule, weather, a daily quote, and a to-do list.
  2. Hobby Tracker — log workouts, books read, songs learned. Show streaks and stats.
  3. Mini Study Tool — flashcards or quizzes for a class you're taking, generated from your notes.
  4. Your Own Pick — anything you'd actually use. Bonus points for that.

Rule: you have to describe what you want — not write any code yourself.

The Concept

From Chatbot to Coworker

A chatbot waits for you to ask. An agent takes a goal and goes after it — using tools, browsing the web, sending emails, running code, all on its own.
